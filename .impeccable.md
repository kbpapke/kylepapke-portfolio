## Design Context

### Users
Hiring managers, engineering leads, recruiters, and technical collaborators reviewing Kyle Papke as a Senior Software Engineer candidate. They arrive with a specific job to do: quickly assess Kyle's technical depth, breadth of experience, and whether his output quality matches the seniority claimed. They are busy and pattern-match fast — the visual credibility of the portfolio itself is evidence of skill.

### Brand Personality
**Bold. Nostalgic. Technically serious.**
Kyle is a 14+ year veteran engineer who started with SPAs in 2011 and now ships agentic AI workflows, Web3 ecosystems, and cybersecurity dashboards. The portfolio should feel like proof-of-work: not a template, a creation. The Habbo Hotel / RPG pixel world aesthetic signals longevity in the craft (you had to be there) while the technical choices (Remix, Vite, D3, Three.js references) signal 2026 fluency.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual tone:** Pixel retro RPG — the Habbo Hotel isometric world as a developer's personal HQ. Dark near-black background with neon lime primary and pixel orange accents. Press Start 2P pixel font for display headings; clean sans-serif for body copy.

**Color palette:**
- Background: `#0D0F0A` (near black)
- Primary: `#39FF14` (neon lime / pixel green)
- Accent: `#FF6B35` (pixel orange)
- Text: `#C8FF96` (green-tinted off-white)
- Card bg: `#141A0F` (dark green panel)
- Wall dark: `#0A120A`
- Tile mid: `#1C2B16`

**References:**
- Habbo Hotel (2003–2010 era): isometric room layout, squat pixel character, warm/vibrant objects on muted tile backgrounds, dark navy UI chrome with gold trim
- RPG games: stat panels, progress bars, level indicators, tooltip-style info panels
- Demoscene aesthetic: procedural art, limited palette, making technical artistry visible

**Anti-references:** Generic ShadCN portfolio clones, "Made with Lovable" templates, LinkedIn-blue color palettes, hero-text-with-headshot layouts.

**Theme:** Dark mode only.

**Typography:**
- Display / pixel headings: `Press Start 2P` (Google Fonts) — used sparingly at key moments
- Body / UI: `Inter` (Google Fonts) — clean, readable at small sizes
- Code blocks: `JetBrains Mono`

**Layout:** Full isometric room as primary navigation metaphor. The room is scrollable and divided into "areas" — Lobby (Hero), Experience Wing, Skills Arcade, Projects Gallery, Claude Lab, Contact Desk. On mobile, degrades to a vertical scroll layout with preserved pixel art panel styling.

### Design Principles
1. **The interface is evidence.** Every design choice should demonstrate frontend capability. Charts, animations, and pixel art are not decoration — they are proof.
2. **Pixel constraints create focus.** Use the limited palette strictly. No gradients except where they enhance depth in isometric faces. No blurs, shadows should be hard/stepped.
3. **Information hierarchy over flash.** Recruiters have 30 seconds. Employer-critical info (title, current company, years of experience, tech stack) must be legible in the first scroll position without interaction.
4. **Responsive isn't an afterthought.** Every component has a defined mobile behavior. Isometric room converts to pixel panel cards on mobile — the aesthetic persists, the layout adapts.
5. **Made with Claude, not despite it.** The footer attribution is a statement. The quality and ambition of the site should make that attribution mean something.
